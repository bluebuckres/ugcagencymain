The A/B Testing Framework That Delivers Results

Step 1: Establish Your Baseline

Most Indian brands jump straight into testing without establishing a control. This is backwards.

Before you test anything:

  • 1. Run current product page (without UGC) for 2 weeks
  • 2. Capture baseline metrics
  • 3. Ensure statistical significance

Key Metrics to Track:

  • • Conversion rate
  • • Average time on page
  • • Bounce rate
  • • Cart abandonment rate
  • • Revenue per visitor

Target Sample Size: Minimum 1,000 visitors per variant for statistical significance

Step 2: Identify Your Testing Variables

A beauty brand in Bangalore tested eight different elements simultaneously and couldn't determine which change drove their 13% conversion lift.

Location-based tests:

  • • Hero section placement
  • • Mid-page vs below fold
  • • Category vs product pages
  • • Mobile vs desktop

Content-type tests:

  • • Photos vs video testimonials
  • • Reviews with/without photos
  • • Instagram UGC vs reviews
  • • Regional vs English content

Presentation tests:

  • • Carousel vs grid layout
  • • Autoplay vs click-to-play
  • • With/without ratings
  • • Lifestyle vs product-only

Step 3: Run Tests With Proper Rigor

Testing Duration Guidelines:

  • High-traffic sites (10,000+ daily): 7-14 days
  • Mid-traffic sites (1,000-10,000): 14-21 days
  • Low-traffic sites (<1,000):< /strong> 21-30 days

Avoid Testing During:

  • • Festival seasons (abnormal traffic)
  • • Major sale events (skewed behavior)
  • • End of month (payday effect)

Your 30-Day Testing Roadmap

Week 1: Foundation

  • • Audit current site for UGC opportunities
  • • Install analytics tracking
  • • Collect UGC with small incentives (₹200-500 discount codes)
  • • Research competitor UGC formats

Week 2: First Test Setup

  • • Choose ONE high-impact variable
  • • Set up testing tool (Google Optimize, VWO, Crazy Egg)
  • • Create mobile-optimized variants
  • • Launch test (aim for 1,000+ visitors per variant)

Week 3: Monitor & Gather Data

  • • Check tests daily but don't make decisions yet
  • • Record qualitative observations
  • • Document external factors (sales, holidays)
  • • Take screenshots of variants

Week 4: Analysis & Next Planning

  • • Analyze with 95% confidence threshold
  • • Implement winning variant
  • • Plan next test based on learnings
  • • Share results to build testing culture
